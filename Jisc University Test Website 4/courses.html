Welcome to Jisc University

A place for knowledge, discovery, and growth.

Courses on offer at the University of Jisc:

Arts and Humanities


English Literature: This course explores the history, theory, and practice of English literature from its earliest beginnings to the present day. Students will study a wide range of texts, including novels, poems, plays, and essays.
History: This course examines the past from a variety of perspectives, including political, social, economic, and cultural. Students will study a range of topics, such as the history of the British Empire, the American Civil War, and the French Revolution.
Philosophy: This course explores the fundamental questions of human existence, such as the nature of reality, the meaning of life, and the relationship between mind and body. Students will study a variety of philosophical traditions, including Western philosophy, Eastern philosophy, and feminist philosophy.
Theology: This course explores the nature of God, the meaning of faith, and the relationship between religion and society. Students will study a variety of religious traditions, including Christianity, Islam, and Judaism.

Social Sciences


Economics: This course examines the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. Students will study a range of topics, such as microeconomics, macroeconomics, and international economics.
Politics: This course examines the nature of power, the role of the state, and the relationship between citizens and government. Students will study a range of topics, such as political theory, comparative politics, and international relations.
Psychology: This course explores the human mind and behavior. Students will study a range of topics, such as cognitive psychology, social psychology, and developmental psychology.
Sociology: This course examines the structure and dynamics of human societies. Students will study a range of topics, such as social inequality, social change, and social institutions.

Sciences


Biology: This course explores the living world, from the smallest cells to the largest ecosystems. Students will study a range of topics, such as cell biology, genetics, and evolution.
Chemistry: This course explores the composition, structure, and properties of matter. Students will study a range of topics, such as atomic theory, organic chemistry, and biochemistry.
Physics: This course explores the fundamental laws of nature, such as gravity,electromagnetism,and quantum mechanics. Students will study a range of topics, such as mechanics, thermodynamics, and optics.
Mathematics: This course explores the language of mathematics and its applications to a variety of fields, such as science, engineering, and economics. Students will study a range of topics, such as algebra, calculus, and statistics.

Engineering


Civil Engineering: This course prepares students for careers in the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure, such as roads, bridges, and buildings. Students will study a range of topics, such as structural engineering, hydraulic engineering, and environmental engineering.
Mechanical Engineering: This course prepares students for careers in the design, construction, and maintenance of mechanical systems, such as engines, machines, and robots. Students will study a range of topics, such as thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and machine design.

Medicine


Medicine: This course prepares students for careers in medicine, such as doctors, nurses, and pharmacists. Students will study a range of topics, such as anatomy, physiology, and pharmacology.
